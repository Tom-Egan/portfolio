Executive Summary

This project focused on redefining how global vehicle controls are accessed and used, integrating into a highly constrained automotive infotainment system. Working across UX, research, and engineering, I helped explore and evaluate multiple interaction models, balancing usability, safety, and platform consistency. The outcome established scalable interaction patterns that shipped on the 24MY Range Rover Velar and rolled out to all JLR vehicle programs.

Setting expectations: This case study highlights UX and interaction design decisions under constraints, rather than final, highly polished visual design.

Section Two

Problem Statement

For the 24MY (2024 Model Year) mid-cycle refresh, Jaguar Land Rover removed a number of physical controls as part of a redesigned interior approach. This resulted in several mission-critical vehicle functions needing to be safely and intuitively relocated into the touchscreen.

As part of a small UX design team, I contributed to re-architecting the existing Pivi infotainment system, adhering to strict legal, functional, and development constraints. Through multiple design iterations, interactive prototypes, and user tests, we delivered a production-ready solution that launched on the Range Rover Velar and later scaled across all JLR vehicles for 24MY and beyond.

Objective

The objective was to provide fast, low-distraction access to high-frequency, safety-critical vehicle controls within the Pivi touchscreen. Affected features included:

  • Climate/HVAC
  • Heated/cooled seats
  • Volume
  • Drive modes
  • Defrost
  • Various vehicle settings (e.g. Auto Stop/Start)
