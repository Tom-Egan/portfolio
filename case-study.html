Summary

  • Company

  • Problem

    Physical vehicle controls removed, requiring safety-critical functions to move into the touchscreen UI

  • Constraints

    Legal one-touch-activation requirements, limited sidebar space, modifying existing software architecture

  • Role

    UX Designer — UX concepting, interaction design, prototyping, usability testing

  • Team

    2 UX Designers + UX Research (US & UK)

  • Platform

    Pivi / Pivi Pro (embedded vehicle software)

  • Key Solutions

    Contextual sidebar buttons, overlays, multi-state controls, quick-adjust drag interactions, pre-trip homescreen tile

  • Outcome

    Shipped on 24MY Range Rover Velar; design and interaction patterns scaled across all Jaguar Land Rover vehicles

Executive Summary

This project focused on redefining how global vehicle controls are accessed and used, integrating into a highly constrained automotive infotainment system. Working across UX, research, and engineering, I helped explore and evaluate multiple interaction models, balancing usability, safety, and platform consistency. The outcome established scalable interaction patterns that shipped on the 24MY Range Rover Velar and rolled out to all JLR vehicle programs.

Setting expectations: This case study highlights UX and interaction design decisions under constraints, rather than final, highly polished visual design.

Design Impact

  • Platform Scalability

    Created a shared interaction model for global controls, used across all vehicle programs and multiple vehicle configurations.

  • Risk Management

    Helped align UX, engineering, and management on interaction tradeoffs early with extension explorations, reducing late-stage rework.

  • Pioneering

    Established a design precedent within JLR for touchscreen-based climate and vehicle controls, and internal ways of working (rapid, iterative design sprints).
